Surrogate Species

Conserving landscape-scale habitats on a species-by-species basis is impractical. Instead we can target conservation of a select group of "surrogate species" while still supporting habitat for a wide range of species.

Here we show how targeting single species at different levels affects the number of species with overlapping habitat. For example, we see that representing 20% of the Bull Trout habitat also represents habitat for 40 different species.
